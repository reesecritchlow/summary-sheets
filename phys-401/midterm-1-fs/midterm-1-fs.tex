    $\ds\int \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d}{dt} \int \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} \implies E_1^\parallel - E_2^\parallel = 0$\\
    $\ds\frac{1}{\mu}\left(\vec{\nabla} \times \vec{B}\right) = \epsilon \pfrac{E}{t} \implies \frac{1}{\mu_1}B_1^\parallel = \frac{1}{\mu_2}B_2^\parallel$\\
    $\ds \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{B} = 0 \implies B_1^\perp = B_2^\perp$\\
    $\ds \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{D} = 0 \implies \epsilon_1 E_1^\perp = \epsilon_2 E_2^\perp$
